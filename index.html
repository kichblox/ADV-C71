places you must visit

mom kitchen

i visited in december 27 2021 very epic

I visited on december 27 2021. The food there is very good and it is a very small little cafe.What lovely people and wonderful food. My husband and I sat at the counter in front of the kitchen, so we could see all that was going on with some of the people preparing and serving the food. It was a great experience. The food itself was so fresh and good. We both and the tomato bisque soup. He had half Reuben and I had the Monterrey Melt. Everything there is organic and locally grown. We split a piece of chocolate pie for dessert. The plants and flowers around the restaurant were inspiring, especially the kale. We loved looking at all the places where they make furniture, leather, pottery, fabric, cheese, the general store and the garden store. I wish we didn't live so far away. We look forward to a return visit.

india place

i visited in december 26 1998 very big epic

Jantar Mantar is an open-air observatory and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is distinctive in that it houses the world’s largest sundial. Fourteen major geometric devices are found at the observatory that do everything from predicting eclipses to tracking the location of stars as the earth orbits around the sun. An interest in astronomy and some knowledge of solid geometry is helpful to fully appreciate this unique observatory built around 1728.

  • Antagonistic Truck Drivers

    Coal rolling is done by forcing more diesel fuel into the engine than the engine can handle, coordinating its release to get as much attention as possible. The coal rollers target eco-friendly cars, the Prius in particular, assuming that the drivers of such cars are liberal-leaning and are therefore likely to be most offended. On public roads, this hobby is illegal, because a truck emitting that much smoke violates the Clean Air Act.

  • New Nuclear Power Plants

    TerraPower recently announced plans to build its Natrium reactor at a retiring coal plant in Kemmerer, Wyoming. This is an incredible opportunity for the state, which currently generates almost 90% of its electricity generation from fossil fuels. The Natrium design represents the future of advanced nuclear reactor technology and is well-suited to provide clean and efficient power to communities across the United States, including the Mountain West.
